# personalization-research-framework

Use this skill to structure prospect research. Stop browsing aimlessly;
target these specific data buckets to build relevant premises.

## The 6 Research Buckets (Ranked by Impact)

### 1. Self-Authored Content (The Gold Mine)
What they explicitly say they care about.
*   **Sources:** LinkedIn posts, Twitter/X posts, podcast interviews, blog articles they wrote.
*   **Angle:** "Listened to your podcast on X. Agree with your take on Y..."
*   **Strength:** Highest. You are validating their public thoughts.

### 2. Engaged Content
What they are interacting with.
*   **Sources:** Comments on LinkedIn posts, webinar attendance, event RSVPs.
*   **Angle:** "Saw you commenting on [Influencer]'s post about [Topic]..."
*   **Strength:** Very High. Shows you spend time in their exact community.

### 3. Self-Identified Traits
How they describe themselves and their focus.
*   **Sources:** LinkedIn "About" section, current job headline, GitHub bio.
*   **Angle:** "Noticed in your bio you're heavily focused on migrating to X..."
*   **Strength:** High. You actually read their profile.

### 4. Company-Level Signals
Events happening at their organization that create pain.
*   **Sources:** Press releases, 10-K reports (for public companies), new open job roles, funding news.
*   **Angle:** "Saw you're hiring 5 new SDRs. Usually that means..."
*   **Strength:** High (if tied to their specific department).

### 5. Professional Background
Their path to their current role.
*   **Sources:** Past job history, time in current role, previous companies.
*   **Angle:** "Saw you were at [Previous Company] when they scaled X..."
*   **Strength:** Medium. Good for rapport, harder to tie to a business pain.

### 6. Personal Data (The Danger Zone)
Non-work related information.
*   **Sources:** Alma mater, hobbies, location, sports teams.
*   **Angle:** "Go Dawgs..." or "Hope you're surviving the snow in Chicago."
*   **Strength:** Low. Can come across as creepy or a waste of time unless done playfully as a PS. Cannot carry a business premise.

## Research Timeboxing

**Never spend more than 3-5 minutes researching a single prospect.**
If you cannot find a strong hook in Bucket 1-4 within 3 minutes, fall back to a highly segmented, persona-based template. Do not waste 15 minutes looking for a college sports angle.

## Output Contract
When advising on research, provide:
- The prioritized bucket to look in based on the prospect's role
- Specific places to look (e.g., "Check their last 3 LinkedIn comments")
- How to transition from the observation to the business pain
